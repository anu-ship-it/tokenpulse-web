If you've ever hit a wall mid-conversation on Claude and had no idea why, you've run into Claude's rate limit system. It's more nuanced than a simple daily cap — and understanding the two layers is the difference between planning your work around it and getting blindsided.

## The Two-Tier System

Claude uses two independent rate limits that reset on different schedules:

**5-Hour Session Limit**
This is the short-term window. Claude tracks how many tokens (input + output) you've consumed over the last 5 hours. When you hit this limit, you can't send new messages until the window resets. The reset time is rolling — it's 5 hours from when you *started*, not from midnight.

**7-Day Weekly Limit**
This is the longer-term cap. Claude tracks cumulative usage over the past 7 days. This limit is shared across all your conversations across all projects. It resets on a weekly basis.

Both limits operate independently. You can exhaust your 5-hour session without touching your 7-day limit. You can also be under your 5-hour limit but still get blocked if your 7-day usage is maxed out.

## What Triggers a Rate Limit?

Every message you send counts — both the tokens in your prompt and the tokens in Claude's response. Long conversations, large file uploads, and detailed outputs all burn quota faster.

The models with the highest capability (Opus) burn quota faster than lighter models (Haiku). If you're hitting limits frequently, switching to a faster model for simple tasks saves your Opus quota for the work that actually needs it.

## How to Stop Getting Cut Off

1. **Watch your 5-hour window before long sessions.** If you're about to start a 2-hour debugging session, check your session percentage first. If you're at 70%, you might not make it through.

2. **Use faster models for simple tasks.** Formatting, summarizing, and rewriting don't need Opus. Save the premium quota for complex reasoning.

3. **Start new chats when topics change.** Every message in a thread adds to context. Carrying old context into a new task wastes tokens on both sides.

4. **Ask Claude to compress context.** When a conversation is getting long, ask: *"Summarize the key decisions and conclusions in under 200 words."* Then start a new chat with just that summary.

5. **Set notifications before you hit 100%.** Knowing at 75% gives you time to wrap up. Knowing at 100% means you're already blocked.

## The Reset Schedule

Neither limit resets at midnight. Both are rolling windows:

- **5-hour limit**: resets 5 hours after the conversation that pushed you over started
- **7-day limit**: resets 7 days after the activity that consumed the quota

This means your reset time changes every time you have a heavy session. A countdown timer that shows exactly when you'll be unblocked is far more useful than a generic "try again later" message.
